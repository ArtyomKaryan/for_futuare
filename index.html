Financial Definitions – Part 1

  • Budget: A financial plan that outlines expected income and expenses over a specific period of time.
  • Income: The total amount of money received from various sources such as salary, investments, or business activities.
  • Expenses: The costs incurred for goods, services, or obligations during a certain period.
  • Financial Planning: The process of setting financial goals and creating strategies to achieve them.
  • Savings: The portion of income not spent and set aside for future use.
  • Financial Discipline: Managing money responsibly to ensure that spending does not exceed income.
  • Priority-Based Budget: Spending is allocated based on the importance of each category or need.
  • Budget Deficit: A financial situation where expenses exceed income.
  • Budget Surplus: A financial situation where income exceeds expenses.
  • Personal Budget: A plan for managing an individual’s income and expenses.
  • Emergency Fund: A savings reserve for unexpected expenses like job loss or medical issues.

Financial Definitions – Part 2

  • Financial Fraud: Intentional deception involving financial transactions for personal gain.
  • Fixed Expenses: Regular costs that don’t vary, such as rent or insurance.
  • Variable Expenses: Costs that change each month, like groceries or bills.
  • Cash Flow: The movement of money in and out over a given period.
  • Debt Management: Strategies for handling and repaying debt effectively.
  • Net Worth: The value of assets minus liabilities.
  • Investment: Allocating money to earn returns through profit or interest.
  • Credit Score: A number showing a person’s creditworthiness based on history.
  • Financial Literacy: The ability to understand and apply financial concepts.
  • Overspending: Spending more than your available budget or income.
  • Passive Income: Income earned with minimal effort, like from investments.
